Biography

Vishal Chourasia is a PhD researcher whose work focuses on secure high-level synthesis (HLS), hardware IP protection, and hardware Trojan analysis for modern VLSI and embedded systems. His research addresses the growing security challenges faced by CAD-driven and HLS-based hardware design flows, particularly in the context of machine learning accelerators, convolutional neural networks (CNNs), and consumer-electronics intellectual property (IP) cores.

His doctoral research has made substantial contributions to the field of hardware watermarking and IP protection, introducing multi-layer, multi-modal, and identity-driven watermarking frameworks embedded directly at the behavioral and scheduling stages of HLS. These methodologies integrate cryptographic primitives, biometric traits, and bio-inspired signatures to bind hardware designs uniquely to their legitimate vendors, thereby countering IP piracy, overbuilding, cloning, and malicious reuse while preserving design quality.

He has also co-authored multiple papers in high-impact international journals, including Nature Scientific Reports, IEEE Computer Society IT Professional, IEEE Embedded Systems Letters, Nature Scientific Reports and IET Computers & Digital Techniques. His journal publications span key advances such as proteogenomic biomarkers and DNA-mimicking fingerprints for HLS watermarking, classification and detection of hardware Trojans in CAD-based IP designs, malevolent HLS (M-HLS) frameworks, and multi-variable encoded scheduling for transient fault-detectable and secure IPs. His work also exposes backdoor Trojans and security vulnerabilities during machine-learning accelerator design phases, contributing to the development of trustworthy AI hardware.

He has also co-authored numerous papers in leading IEEE international conferences, including SOCC, ICCE-Taiwan, iSES, VDAT, ICM, and ICFTSS, focusing on key-driven encrypted HLS watermarking and evolutionary design space exploration techniques for security-aware hardware optimization under the supervision of Prof Anirban Sengupta.

A key focus of his research is hardware Trojan threats in HLS environments. He is a co-author of forthcoming IET book chapters (2026), led by Prof. Anirban Sengupta, which analyze malicious HLS frameworks and Trojan vulnerabilities in HLS-based machine-learning accelerators, along with corresponding countermeasures.
